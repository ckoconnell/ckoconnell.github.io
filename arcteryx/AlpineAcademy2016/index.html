  • Mountain Clean-Up Day

    Clinic Meta

    Let's give back to the mountains we love. We are calling participants of the Alpine Academy 2015 to join certified mountain guides and over 20 international Arc'teryx athletes for a Mountain Clean-Up Day.

    This initiative is a collaboration between the Arc'teryx Alpine Academy and the Compagnie du Mont-Blanc (CMB) to help keep our mountains pure. So come out to support this Mountain Clean-Up Day, while getting acclimatized at around 2000m to the beautiful Chamonix environment.

    All voluntary participants will be provided a free lift pass by the CMB to get to the designated area as well as a lunch package sponsored by LYOfood.

  • Acclimatization

    Clinic Meta

    To get you prepared and acclimatized for our weekend clinics and the high alpine environment, we offer an easy walking traverse from Plan de l'Aiguille to Montenvers.

    This tour is advised for all participants who have not been active above 3000m during the previous four weeks.

  • Glacier preparation

    Clinic Meta

    This clinic focuses on walking with crampons and using an ice axe. You will learn about roping up and basic techniques for glacier walking.

    This day is mandatory for participants who have never worn crampons before and/or would like to continue with their technical mountaineering education or glacier walk.

  • Suunto Navigation

    Clinic Meta

    Learn all about skilled navigation and orienteering in the mountains. Clinic topics include:

    • How to plan and prepare for a tour
    • How to read maps, how to read GPS coordinates on the map / different grids / Google earth
    • Altitude lines on maps
    • How does an altimeter work? Why calibrate?
    • When to use barometer – when altimeter?
    • Navigation with altimeter & compass
    • Back up GPS – store the route on various advices
    • Weather monitoring/forecast with barometer / storm alarm

  • First Aid and Basic Rescue

    Clinic Meta

    This course is all about high alpine rescue and on how to behave in an emergency. This course shows you various rescue scenarios and teaches you from how to get help on the mountain to crevasse rescue techniques. It also provides some knowledge on preventing accidents and how to avoid the fall. This will be given by local specialists from La Chamonniard

  • Basic Mountaineering Education

    Clinic Meta

    This course will prepare you for basic glacier tours and easy alpine climbs. This includes: roping up on glaciers, basic crevasse rescu scenarios, ice axe techniques, snow and ice anchors, self-arrest, route planning and advice on equipment.

    This course is mandatory for everyone who has never done a glacier tour before

  • Advanced Technical Alpinism

    Clinic Meta

    This course will further your knowledge and skills to prepare you for different situations and mixed terrain in the mountains. You will practice walking on exposed terrain and ridges, learn how to adjust the rope system accordingly (short roping). You will be shown different ice axe uses and will practice front point technique. More skills, such as setting up an abseil, placing protection and leading the group on your own will be covered in this course.

  • Multi Pitch Rock Climbing

    Clinic Meta

    This course is taking your rock climbing knowledge into a multi-pitch environment. This clinic teaches all the required skills for leading and seconding bolted sport climbing routes, guidebook use, terminology, equipment needs, climbing calls, abseiling techniques, building anchors.

  • Glacier Tour of Vallee Blanche

    Clinic Meta

    The Geant glacier definitely presents one of the most spectacular and exciting panoramas in the world. A tour on the Vallee Blanche takes you right into the heart of the history of alpinism. One of the most breathtaking high altitude sceninc hikes in Europe!

  • Hilleberg Overnight Bivy Course

    Clinic Meta

    This course offers the unique possibility of camping overnight bivy in the Mont-Blanc Massif with two world-class Arc’teryx athletes. Before going onto the glacier, you will prepare for a night on the snow. Later on you will learn essential campcraft skills such as cooking outside, finding the right location and experience cold and snowy environment. World-class alpinists will be there to teach bivouac basics and share their alpine experiences.

    You will also learn how to build an emergency snow cave and wind walls to protect the tent in case of a storm.

    Tents will be supplied by Hilleberg.

  • Alpine Photography

    Clinic Meta

    Would you like to improve your photography skills in the mountains?

    You will go up the mountain on the last lift or by foot, shoot the last light of the day and have the chance to shoot special settings during sunset. Not to worry though, as you will be staying in the nearby hut and discussing the photos of the day and learning more about mountain photography. The next morning will get up early for sunrise, then shoot some climbing and mountaineering action.

  • Traditional Climbing

    Clinic Meta

    This "traditional" Alpine Climbing clinic offers experienced climbers some insights into what we also call "Trad Climbing", where there is no need of clipping bolts.

    You will learn and deepen your knowledge about how to place gear, make belays in various terrains, use of modern safety tools and climbing techniques. Learn from the experts!

  • High Altitude Medicine

    Clinic Meta

    This course by Ifremmont will discuss what could happen on a high altitude expedition. You will learn how to prepare for alpine adventures, how to deal with mountain sickness "MAM" and the use of the "decompression chamber".

    In addition, you will also learn 5 different techniques on how to immobilize an injury and how to survive in extreme conditions. The end of the day will be filled with making a survival sledge to transport a casualty.

  • Dry Tooling and Mixed Climbing

    Clinic Meta

    An exciting alternative to regular rock climbing and a great way to learn and practice mixed climbing skills.

    Dry tooling involves the use of ice axes and rock shoes to ascend climbs that are not covered by snow or ice. During this clinic you will learn how to prepare best for your mixed climbing.

  • Ice Climbing, Level 1

    Clinic Meta

    This clinic gives you an introduction to the ice climbing world. Equipment, leashless tools, how to build an Abalakov anchor, ice climbing strategies, safety, ice terminology as well as route selection, ice grades and your first moves on the ice will be covered here.

  • Improvised rescue on alpine climbs

    Clinic Meta

    Got stuck on a route? This is a rock rescue course, which also applies to alpine climbs. As a climber, you need to be self-sufficient and prepared to improvise rescue scenarios.

    You will learn how to prepare for rescue situations, escape the climbing system, perform raises, lowers and self rescue. We will also cover rope ascending techniques, reversing certain belay methods, and safe decision making. We will also discuss the implications of carrying out a rescue in technical terrain.

  • Grade 7+ Rock climbing techniques

    Clinic Meta

    7+ is a workshop designed to improve your sport climbing technique. Climbing involves a variety of moves (heel hooks, drop knees, cross, or undercling) to be executed in complex combinations.

    Through demonstration, discussion of principles, and feedback on your performances, experts will help you widen your move repertory and deepen your understanding and experience of climbing.

    The requirements for the workshop are:

    • 5 year of experiences, with current and frequent climbing on cliffs at a 6c grade or more.
    • Complete autonomy for belay and other safety techniques outdoors
    • And obviously, an open mind...

  • Ice Climbing for Women

    Clinic Meta

    This women-only clinic focuses on ice climbing and the lifestyle that comes with it.

    This clinic gives you an introduction to the ice climbing world. Equipment, leashless tools, how to build an Abalakov anchor, ice climbing strategies, safety, ice terminology as well as route selection, ice grades will be covered here.

  • Women's Glacier Tour

    Clinic Meta

    This women-only clinic is designed to inspire women to a greater sense of self-sufficiency in a relaxed environment.

    Together with a female guide you will experience a spectacular glacier tour. It's all about being surrounded in the alpine environment, sharing your experiences with other women and learn how to make use of your equipment.

  • Big Wall Climbing

    Clinic Meta

    This Big Wall clinic is an introduction into learning different techniques for climbing long routes involving the use of portaledges. This focuses on hauling techniques, how to jumar up a rope, and general technical differences to classic multi pitch routes. Security is the most important issue here.

    Topics will include: technical climbs with two etriers, how to place cams and nuts how to use sky hooks, crag hooks, placements of pitons, make traverses in long stretches, build a belay, abseil and how to leave a big wall.

  • Ski Alpinism

    Clinic Meta

    This Introduction to Ski Alpinism clinic offers experienced skiers some insights into various techniques how to perform in steep skiing environments. This includes how to learn the right techniques of skinning up alpine terrains by using kick turns as well as skiing and abseil down steep snowy terrain.

    You will practice to choose the right moment to put on/remove skis and use crampons on a steep slope. You will learn how to make an anchor in mixed terrain, how to make a ski belay and how to pass steep rock or ice sections. Requirements: Experience on skiing 35 degrees or more, knowledge on avalanche safety as well as experience in technical mountaineering.

  • Bouldering

    Clinic Meta

    Love bouldering? Or want to develop your skills on hard moves to transfer into routes? This clinic will take you bouldering in the beautiful Chamonix valley where our team will help you hone your climbing and movement skills.

    With loads of boulders to try and climb we will look at various techniques and methods to move your bouldering to the next level. We will work on boulder problems developing skills such as footwork, use of heel hooks and toe hooks, using momentum, dynamic movement and problem reading.

    You will also learn effective warm up and warm down techniques and take away some exercise tools for improvement. Participants need no prior bouldering experience, this clinic will be suitable for all levels.

  • Splitboarding

    Clinic Meta

    This Introduction to Splitboarding clinic offers experienced snowboarders some insights into various techniques how to perform in steep skiing environments.

    This includes how to learn the right techniques of skinning up alpine terrains by using kick turns as well as snowboarding and abseil down steep snowy terrain.

    You will practice to choose the right moment to put on/remove splitboard and use crampons on a steep slope. You will learn how to make an anchor in mixed terrain, how to make a ski belay and how to pass steep rock or ice sections. Requirements: Experience on skiing 35 degrees or more, knowledge on avalanche safety as well as experience in technical mountaineering.
